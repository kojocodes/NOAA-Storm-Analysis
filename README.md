 Which Storms Are Actually the Most Dangerous? 

The Big Idea
Weather reports usually focus on the "scary" stuff like tornadoes, but I wanted to see what the data actually says. I looked at over 75 years of US storm records (from 1950 all the way to 2026) to figure out two things:
1. Which weather events actually hurt the most people?
2. Which ones do the most damage to our wallets and cities?

## What I Learned
* **Heat is the "Silent Killer":** Even though tornadoes get all the news coverage, the data shows that **extreme heatwaves** are actually a leading cause of death. We should probably be focusing more on keeping people cool.
* **Water is the Most Expensive:** If you want to know where the money goes, look at **floods and hurricanes.** They cause the most massive repair bills, meaning we need to build better defenses in coastal areas.

## How I Built This
Language: I used **R** (a coding language for math and data).
The Messy Part: Real-world data is messy. I had to write code that turned weird shortcuts like "K" (thousands) and "B" (billions) into real numbers so I could add them all up.
Visuals:I made charts to make the "boring" numbers easy to see at a glance.
